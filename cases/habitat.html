Habitat

Habitat is a concept for a web and mobile app for tracking pollution levels that I have created as part of the Google UX Design program.
The following case study will present my whole process from Ideation to Prototyping.

Brief

Habitat is an app that tracks pollution levels and the environmental disasters that take place in or near my hometown of Filiasi, Romania.

the problem

As cities develop the pollution levels increase. Combined with unfortunate events such as fires this will lead to us living in unsafe conditions without us not even knowing that the air we breathe or the water we drink might be contaminated.

the goal

The goal of the project is for the habitants of my hometown of Filiasi, Romania to be able to track the quality of the air and water in the area. Additionally, they will be able to report any environmental issues such as fires.

Role

UX Designer

methods

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

Duration

November - December 2021

tool(s)

Adobe XD

Understanding the user

During this phase I did user research, developed personas, problem statements and a competitive audit. The next step was a quick ideation exercise.

The research made very clear that users want to know how safe the environment they live in is and are willing to report environmental disasters if they had access to an easy-to-use tool to help guide them.

Starting the design

During this phase I designed the digital wireframes and the low-fidelity prototypes for both the web app and the dedicated mobile app.

These designs focused on delivering a way to filter the data and avoid overwhelming the users. The mobile app displays the same information as the desktop app, but with an interface more suitable for the smaller screen size.

Accessibility considerations

Clear labels for interactive elements that can be read by screen readers.

The focus on the home screen help define the primary task or action for the user.

The speech-to-text feature allows users that can’t use a standard keyboard fully use the functions of the search box.

Takeaways

impact

Users shared that the app helped them better understand the health dangers they might be facing due to high pollution levels.

what i learned

I learned that even though the problem I was trying to solve was a big one, diligently going through each step of the design process and aligning with specific user needs helped me come up with solutions that were both feasible and useful.
