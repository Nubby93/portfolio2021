doosio

doosio is a concept for a bookkeeping app for a local restaurant in my hometown that I have created as part of the Google UX Design program. The project promp was generated using Sharpen, the Design Challenge Generator.
The following case study will present my whole process from Ideation to Prototyping.

Brief

the problem

Bookkeeping apps tend to be bulky and hard to use. We want to solve that problem by creating an app with a simple and intuitive, but powerful interface.

the goal

The goal of the app is to be a one stop shop for managing a businessâ€™ bookkeeping to save time and energy for the users.

Role

UX Designer

methods

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

Duration

September - October 2021

tool(s)

Figma

Understanding the user

During this phase I did user research, developed personas, user journeys, problem statements and a competitive audit. The next step was a quick ideation exercise.

The research made very clear that users want a simple app that is easy to understand and fast to use.

Starting the design

During this phase I designed the digital wireframes and the low-fidelity prototypes for the app.

These designs focused on delivering a homepage packed with valuable information and quick acess to the most important features via the floating button.

Refining the design

During this phase I designed the mockups, high-fidelity prototypes and accessibility considerations.

Mockups of the dedicated mobile app (light & dark mode)



Accessibility considerations

A good contrast between the elements in the page makes the app easy to navigate for both low-sighted and colour-blind users.

Each action will give user feedback on the outcome of the action.

Images have alt properties and we used semantic HTML which is needed for screen readers and AT.

Takeaways

impact

The app impacts small business owners that do not possess large resources and staff.

what i learned

Throughout the project I have learned about all the stages of a UX Research study and about the bookkeeping needs of a restaurant.
